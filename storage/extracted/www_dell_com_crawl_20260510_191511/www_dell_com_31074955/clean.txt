What is Cyber Resilience?
Cyber Resilience involves safeguarding information, ensuring recoverability, availability, and complying with regulations. It includes measures like backup and restore, data immutability, and long-term retention, functions.
Why Cyber Resilience is important?
Cyber resilience is crucial because it:
Ensures Data Availability:
Allows information that has been protected to be recovered in the event of any data loss including system failure, human error, corruption or a cyber-attack.
Preserves Fundamental Rights:
Laws like the EU GDPR recognise cyber resilience as a right.
Builds Trust:
Effective protection fosters confidence with customers and partners.
Enhances Brand Reputation:
Demonstrating commitment to data protection improves an organisation’s image.
Provides Cyber Resilience:
Ensures critical data can be recovered in the event of a cyber-attack to resume business operations.
Saves Time and Costs:
Proper protection minimises downtime and resource loss.
