SBI: A better bank, or a better valued one?
Agencies
Synopsis
Here is a question: Is SBI a structurally better bank, or the same bank priced for a benign cycle that has run unusually well? Through this year, India’s largest lender has anchored itself to specific lines on margin, credit growth, asset quality, returns, and capital. A meaningful portion of the year’s strength has been carried by items that will not return next year: A partial stake sale in another bank, a special dividend from a subsidiary, a steady run of recoveries from written-off accounts. The Q4 numbers are the year-end check. This piece gives investors a usable scorecard: What a better bank looks like, and what a better valued one looks like – on each line of the result.
Like all other PSU banks, SBI’s stock has seen a re-rating in the last few years. The market has decided this is no longer a typical PSU bank. The question is whether the franchise underneath the price has actually improved, or whether a benign cycle and a few helpful one-offs have lifted a year and the multiple has run with it.
